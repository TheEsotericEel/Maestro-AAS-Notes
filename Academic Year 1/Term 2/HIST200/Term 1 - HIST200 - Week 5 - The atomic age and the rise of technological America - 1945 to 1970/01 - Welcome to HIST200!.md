# Welcome to HIST200

## 1. Lesson Focus
Course introduction covering structure, grading, unit topics, and weekly workflow for HIST200: American History Since World War II.

## 2. Course Structure
**Duration**: 2 weeks total, split into 2 units
**Weekly Rhythm**: Lessons → Unit review at end of each week
**Final Week**: Lessons → Weekly review + final comprehensive course review

## 3. Grading Breakdown
**Weekly Unit Reviews**: 50% of course grade (combined from both units)
**Final Comprehensive Review**: 50% of course grade (end of Unit 2)

**Bonus Points**:
- Up to +10 points for on-time reviews
- Up to +10 points for on-time discussion posts

## 4. Unit Topics

**Unit 1**:
- Post-World War II power shift and the nuclear age
- Early Cold War technology, fear politics, and consumer growth

**Unit 2**:
- Economic crises and public opinion in the Vietnam era
- Market liberalism, personal computing, and digital networks
- Globalization, new security state, and the rise of mobile & AI transformations

## 5. Historical Context for Technology
Post-1945 developments directly shaped modern technology:
- Nuclear weapons
- Cold War
- Consumer technology
- Early digital age

## 6. Open-Note Review
- 2 units over 2 weeks
- Each week: lessons then unit review
- Weekly reviews = 50%, final review = 50%
- Bonus points available for on-time submissions
- Unit 1: WWII to early Cold War
- Unit 2: Vietnam era to modern AI era

## 7. Final Takeaway
HIST200 covers American history from 1945 to present, connecting historical events to modern technology—structured as 2 weekly units with reviews worth 100% of the grade plus bonus points.
