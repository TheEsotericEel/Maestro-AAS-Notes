# Prospect Theory and Risk

> "The coin has no memory. The market has no mercy."

## 1. Prospect Theory (Kahneman & Tversky, 1979)
### The Death of Rational Utility
Classical economics assumes we are rational: \$100 is worth \$100.
**Prospect Theory** proves we are irrational:
1.  **Loss Aversion**: Losses hurt **2.25x** more than equivalent gains feel good.
    -   *Pain of losing \$100 > Joy of finding \$100.*
2.  **Diminishing Sensitivity**: The difference between \$100 and \$200 feels huge. The difference between \$1,100 and \$1,200 feels small.

## 2. The Reflection Effect
We change our risk profile depending on whether we are winning or losing.
-   **In the Gains (Winning)**: We become **Risk Averse**. "Lock it in!" (Selling winning stocks too early).
-   **In the Losses (Losing)**: We become **Risk Seeking**. "Double or nothing!" (Holding losing stocks hoping they bounce back).

### Engineering Trap: The Sunk Cost Fallacy
Because we hate losses, we "throw good money after bad."
-   **Scenario**: You spent 2 weeks taking a bad approach. It's not working.
-   **Rational Choice**: Abandon it.
-   **Prospect Choice**: "I can't lose those 2 weeks. I'll spend 2 more weeks to fix it."
-   **Result**: You lose 4 weeks.

## 3. Practical Protocol: Zero-Based Thinking
To defeat Loss Aversion, reset the reference point.
-   **The Question**: "Knowing what I know now, would I start this project today?"
-   **If No**: Kill it immediately. The past investment is gone.
-   **If Yes**: Continue.

## 4. The Gambler's Fallacy
The belief that if something happens more frequently than normal (e.g., 5 Heads in a row), it will happen less frequently in the future (Tail is "due").
-   **Reality**: Events are independent.
-   **Tech Example**: "The server hasn't crashed in 300 days. It's due for a crash." -> **False**. (Unless it's a memory leak). Reliability does not accrue "debt" of randomness.

## ⚡ Key Takeaway
> **Cut your losses short. Let your winners run.**
> Our brain wants to do the opposite (Keep losses, Sell winners). You must fight your OS.
