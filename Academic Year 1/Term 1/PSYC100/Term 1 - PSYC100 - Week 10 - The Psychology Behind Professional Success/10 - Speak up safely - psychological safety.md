# Speak Up Safely: Psychological Safety

> "If you punish the messenger, you stop receiving the mail."

## 1. Project Aristotle (Google)
### The Discovery
Google studied 180 teams to find the "Perfect Algorithm" for a team.
-   **Hypothesis**: The best teams have the highest combined IQ + PhDs.
-   **Result**: False. The #1 predictor of success was **Psychological Safety**.

### What is Psychological Safety?
It is the belief that you will not be punished or humiliated for speaking up with ideas, questions, concerns, or mistakes.
-   **Unsafe**: "I better not ask, I'll look stupid."
-   **Safe**: "I don't understand this architecture. Can you explain?"

## 2. The Crash Report
In aviation and software, **Reporting Errors** is critical.
-   **Amy Edmondson's Finding**: High-performing teams report *more* errors than low-performing teams.
-   **Why?** They aren't making more mistakes. They are **hiding fewer of them**.
-   **The Code Connection**: If a Junior Dev breaks prod and is terrified, they will hide the logs. The downtime increases. If they are safe, they yell "I broke it!" and the team fixes it in 5 minutes.

## 3. Engineering Application: The Blameless Post-Mortem
When an incident occurs:
-   **Bad Question**: "Who caused the outage?" (Blame -> Defense).
-   **Good Question**: "What context allowed the outage to happen?" (Curiosity -> Solution).
    -   *Example*: "The CI pipeline didn't catch the null pointer. Let's add a linting rule." (System Fix).

## 4. Practical Protocol: Leader Fallibility
If you are a Senior/Lead:
1.  **Admit Ignorance**: "I'm not sure about this design. What do you think?"
    -   *Effect*: Grants permission for others to not know everything.
2.  **Celebrate the Catch**: "Thanks for finding that bug in my code."
    -   *Effect*: Reinforces that truth is more important than hierarchy.

## ⚡ Key Takeaway
> **Fear is the mind-killer.**
> It is also the innovation-killer. You cannot build the future if you are afraid of being wrong.
