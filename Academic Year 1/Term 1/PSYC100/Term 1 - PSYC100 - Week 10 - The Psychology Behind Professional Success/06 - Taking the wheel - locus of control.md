# Taking the Wheel: Locus of Control

> "Grant me the serenity to accept the things I cannot change, courage to change the things I can, and wisdom to know the difference."

## 1. Locus of Control (Julian Rotter)
### What is it?
Where do you believe the power lies in your life?
-   **External Locus (The Passenger)**: "Things happen *to* me." (Victim Mindset).
    -   "The server crashed because AWS sucks."
    -   "I didn't get promoted because the boss plays favorites."
-   **Internal Locus (The Driver)**: "I make things happen." (Owner Mindset).
    -   "The server crashed because I didn't build redundancy."
    -   "I didn't get promoted because I didn't demonstrate enough value."

### The Data
People with Internal Locus:
-   Earn more money.
-   Are promoted faster.
-   Have lower stress (Cortisol) levels.

## 2. The Circles of Influence (Stephen Covey)
Visualize three concentric circles.
1.  **Circle of Concern (Outer)**: Things you care about but cannot change. (The Economy, The Weather, Selecting the CEO).
    -   *Action*: **Ignore**. Worrying here is wasted CPU cycles.
2.  **Circle of Influence (Middle)**: Things you can sway but not control. (Your Team's Morale, Your Boss's Priorities).
    -   *Action*: **Persuade**. Use social capital here.
3.  **Circle of Control (Center)**: Things you own 100%. (Your Code, Your Attitude, Your Skillset).
    -   *Action*: **Execute**. Spend 80% of your energy here.

## 3. Engineering Application: Incident Response
-   **External Dev**: "Who broke the build?" (Blame). "Why is this legacy code so bad?" (Complaint).
-   **Internal Dev**: "How can I fix the build?" (Action). "I will write a wrapper to isolate the legacy code." (Solution).

## 4. Practical Protocol: The Language Audit
Listen to your own voice in meetings.
-   **External**: "I *have to* go to this meeting." -> **Disempowered**.
-   **Internal**: "I *choose to* go to this meeting because I want to align with the team." -> **Empowered**.
-   **External**: "They won't let us use Rust."
-   **Internal**: "I haven't written a convincing enough RFC for Rust yet."

## ⚡ Key Takeaway
> **You are not a victim of the codebase.**
> You are the author of the codebase. If you don't like it, refactor it.
