# Anchoring & Framing Effects

> "He who sets the Anchor, sets the terms of reality."

## 1. The Anchoring Effect
### What is it?
The cognitive bias where an individual depends too heavily on an initial piece of information offered (the "anchor") when making decisions.
-   **The Gravity Well**: Once an anchor is set, all future arguments orbit around it.

### Engineering Danger: Estimation Poker
-   **Scenario**: The Lead Dev says, "I think this is a 3-point ticket."
-   **Result**: The Junior Dev (who thought it was an 8) says "Yeah, maybe a 5."
-   **The Fix**: **Silent Voting**. Everyone writes their estimate down *before* speaking. This kills the Anchor.

## 2. The Framing Effect
### The Lens Changes the Math
How you present data (The Frame) changes the decision, even if the math is identical.
-   **Gain Frame**: "This refactor has a 90% success rate." -> **Reaction**: "Do it!" (Risk Averse).
-   **Loss Frame**: "This refactor has a 10% failure rate." -> **Reaction**: "Too risky!" (Risk Avoidant).

### The Asian Disease Problem (Tversky & Kahneman)
-   **Program A**: "200 people will be saved." (Preferred).
-   **Program B**: "1/3 probability that 600 will be saved, 2/3 probability that no one will be saved."
-   *Note*: A and B are mathematically identical. But "Saved" (Gain) feels better than "Will not be saved" (Loss).

## 3. Practical Protocol: Negotiation Defense
### How to De-Anchor
When a salesperson/recruiter throws a number:
1.  **Recognize It**: "That is an Anchor."
2.  **Reject It**: Do not bargain from their number. "That number is not consistent with the market."
3.  **Counter-Anchor**: Set a new reality.

### How to Frame Arguments
To get your architecture approved:
-   **Don't say**: "If we don't do this, we lose \$10k." (Loss Frame - panic).
-   **Do say**: "Invest \$5k to save \$10k." (Gain Frame - value).
*Actually, for security patches, utilize Loss Aversion: "We are currently leaking \$10k/month." Fear motivates action.*

## ⚡ Key Takeaway
> **Context is Content.**
> The frame you put around the data matters more than the data itself.
