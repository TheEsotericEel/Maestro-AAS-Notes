# The Halo & Horn Effects

> "Charisma is not Competence."

## 1. The Dirty Lens (Thorndike)
### What is it?
The **Halo Effect** is a cognitive bias where the perception of one positive trait influences the perception of other unrelated traits.
-   **Mechanism**: **Attribute Substitution** (Kahneman).
-   **The Error**: "He is tall and confident." -> Brain substitutes -> "He must be smart and a good leader."

### The Horn Effect (The Inverse)
-   **The Error**: "He is messy and stutters." -> Brain substitutes -> "He must be lazy and incompetent."

## 2. Engineering Application: Hiring & Tooling
### The Interview Trap
Interviews are notoriously bad at predicting performance because they are **Halo Hunts**.
-   **Scenario**: Candidate A is charming, confident, and uses buzzwords. Candidate B is nervous, quiet, but writes optimizing compilers for fun.
-   **Result**: We hire A. A destroys the codebase. We rejected B, who would have saved it.

### The "Pretty UI" Trap
-   **Scenario**: A new library has a beautiful landing page (Halo).
-   **Assumption**: "The code must be well-written."
-   **Reality**: It might be spaghetti code wrapped in a CSS framework. Always check the GitHub issues, not just the landing page.

## 3. Practical Protocol: The "Blind" Evaluation
To defeat the Halo:
1.  **Blind Grading**: Review the code sample *before* you meet the candidate. Grade the work, not the face.
2.  **Structured Interviews**: Ask the exact same questions to every candidate. Rate each answer 1-5 immediately. Do not rely on the "Vibe" at the end.
3.  **Devil's Advocate**: If you *love* a candidate, force yourself to list 3 weaknesses. If you *hate* a candidate, list 3 strengths.

## ⚡ Key Takeaway
> **Separate the signal from the packaging.**
> A beautiful box can hold a bomb. A cardboard box can hold a diamond. Open the box.
