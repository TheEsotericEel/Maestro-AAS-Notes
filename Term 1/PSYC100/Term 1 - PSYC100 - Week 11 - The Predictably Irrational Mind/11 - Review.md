# Weekly Review: The Predictably Irrational Mind

## 1. The Narrative Arc
This week, we opened the hood of the human machine and found spaghetti code. We are not logic engines; we are **pattern-matching engines** optimized for survival on the savanna, not for code reviews in the cloud.

## 2. Core Concepts Summary
| Bias | The "Glitch" | The "Patch" |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Heuristics** | "It looks like a duck." (Stereotyping). | **Base Rates**: Look at the boring statistics. |
| **Anchoring** | "First number = God." | **Silent Voting**: Hide estimates until everyone decides. |
| **Prospect Theory** | "Loss hurts 2x." (Risk Aversion). | **Zero-Based Thinking**: "Would I buy this today?" |
| **Sunk Cost** | "I spent 3 weeks on this." | **Future Value**: "Is the *next* hour worth it?" |
| **False Memory** | "I remember clicking X." | **Logs**: Trust the disk, not the brain. |
| **Inattentional Blindness** | "I didn't see the bug." | **Checklists**: Force the spotlight to move. |
| **Dunning-Kruger** | "I know everything." | **Metacognition**: "Have I done this before?" |
| **Halo Effect** | "He looks smart." | **Work Samples**: Evaluate the code, not the face. |

## 3. The Maestro's Toolbelt
-   **The Pre-Mortem**: Simulate failure to prevent it.
-   **The Decision Journal**: Write down *why* you decided X. (Prevents Hindsight Bias).
-   **The Pause**: Mandatory delay for expensive decisions.

## 4. Final Thought
> Being "Smart" does not protect you from Bias.
> In fact, smart people are often *better* at rationalizing their biased decisions (Cognitive Dissonance).
> **Humility and Systems** are your only defense.
