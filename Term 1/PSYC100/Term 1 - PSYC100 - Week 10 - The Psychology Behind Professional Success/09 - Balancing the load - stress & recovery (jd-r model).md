# Balancing the Load: Stress & Recovery (JD-R Model)

> "Burnout is not a badge of honor. It is a system failure."

## 1. The Job Demands-Resources (JD-R) Model
### What is it?
The most validated theory of occupational stress. It treats your energy like a server budget.
-   **Job Demands (The Load)**: Things that cost energy.
    -   *Tech*: Deadlines, buggy code, on-call shifts, emotional labor (dealing with angry clients).
-   **Job Resources (The Capacity)**: Things that give energy/help achieve goals.
    -   *Tech*: Autonomy, good tooling, supportive team, feedback, sleep.

### The Equation
-   **Burnout**: `Demands > Resources` (for a sustained period).
-   **Engagement**: `Resources > Demands` (Flow state).

## 2. Allostatic Load (The Cost of Uptime)
Computers can run at 100% CPU indefinitely (with cooling). Humans cannot.
-   **Acute Stress**: Short burst (Deadline). Good for performance.
-   **Chronic Stress**: Sustained load. Cortisol eats the hippocampus (memory).
-   **The Crash**: You don't "slow down." You break. (Panic attacks, depression, physical illness).

## 3. Engineering Application: Capacity Planning
### For Yourself
1.  **Audit Demands**: Are you fixing bugs, mentoring, *and* interviewing candidates?
2.  **Audit Resources**: Do you have the right tools? Do you have authority?
3.  **Rebalance**: If Demands increase (Sprint Crunch), Resources *must* increase (Free lunch, comp time later) or Quality will decrease.

### Recovery is a Feature, No a Bug
-   **Sleep**: The nightly defrag.
-   **Detach**: You must fully disconnect. Checking Slack "just for a minute" keeps the stress thread ensuring.

## 4. Practical Protocol: The "Shutdown Ritual" (Cal Newport)
At the end of the day:
1.  **Close all tabs**.
2.  **Update the Todo list** for tomorrow (dump the RAM).
3.  **Say "Shutdown Complete"**.
4.  **Do not touch code** until morning.

## ⚡ Key Takeaway
> **You are a biological machine.**
> You have a duty cycle. If you override the safety limits, you void the warranty.
